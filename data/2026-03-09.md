By applying the classical Lie symmetry method, we determine the admitted Lie point symmetries and compute the corresponding infinitesimal generators according to the functional relationship between \( C(u) \) and \( K(u) \). The admitted symmetries are used to reduce the partial differential equation to ordinary differential equations and to construct invariant solutions. Particular cases of physical interest are analyzed in detail, including Storm-type materials and power-law dependence of \( C(u) \) and \( K(u) \) on \( u \). For these cases, similarity solutions are obtained.
